Hi! This is Edy!

This is my short story... :D

I've been immersed in coding since my last year in junior high at SMP Katolik Santu Petrus Kota Pontianak, particularly for competitive programming competitions. During this time, I primarily worked on logical exercises and programming tasks related to data structures. I used Pascal, with Free Pascal IDE, to write solutions for these tasks. My involvement in competitive programming continued throughout my senior years, culminating in my highest achievement as a national finalist in Medan in 2010.

Following my high school graduation, I worked as a math teacher, tutoring senior high students for a couple of years. During my university years, around semester 4 or 5, I joined the university's IT department (UPT Pusat Komputer Universitas Tanjungpura) as a staff member. In this role, I was tasked with developing applications such as E-Office (mostly PHP, jQuery-based web application) for university administration. Although the application was successfully deployed, I wasn't entirely certain about its implementation. Another significant project I undertook during this time was creating a geographic information system to store bridge information for a city in Kalimantan Barat using Delphi.

My career accelerated rapidly from there. In addition to my role as a developer, I volunteered in various developer community activities, including Female Dev Chapter Pontianak, Google Day (Widya Dharma), and IAK (Indonesia Android Kejar) Chapter Pontianak. It was during this period that I crossed paths with the founder of Turbin, the company I currently work for.

Throughout this journey, I've learned many valuable lessons. One of the most significant realizations for me was that technology isn't just about dealing with machines; it's primarily about humans. As developers, we have the important task of serving as translators, creating tools to empower and assist people.
